WHAT IS

THE DIRECT HEALTH ALLIANCE?

Direct Health Alliance is a comprehensive, transparent marketplace for providers,
pharmacies, and other health services to direct connect with patients and employers.

By connecting direct, patients and employers will see substantial cost reductions in every aspect of the healthcare
experience, often up to 90%.

Whether you have a high-deductible plan, health share, or you are currently uninsured, our platform will help you get the most from your healthcare dollar. You’ll also be supporting a system that will fundamentally change the way healthcare works, which translates directly to better quality care, lower cost, and broader access.

Membership Benefits

An ever-growing marketplace of services and procedures from blood labs and imaging to bundled pricing to treat rare cancers, and everything in between, Direct Health Alliance allows connection between provider and patient without the traditional restricted “networks”, unnecessary cost, or other wasteful and tired concepts. By establishing a platform for all providers, both large and small, we’ve created a level playing field designed to optimize the opportunity to create value (price & quality) for you. We believe the greatest benefit to membership is the power to restore the healthcare system to a consumer-driven marketplace and the impact on improving healthcare quality, cost, and access. Ultimately, while you will benefit tremendously by joining Direct Health Alliance, your membership is a vote for a better healthcare system. A few of the specific benefits are outlined below:

Pharmacy

Prescriptions at cost from 70,000 pharmacies across the US. Most 30 day generics are $2 or less, substantially lower than co-pays on typical healthcare plans, and 50% or less than GoodRx. Brand name and specialty drugs are still expensive, but we have eliminated additional mark-ups. For orphan drugs, we work hard to find the best way to buy.

Providers

Thousands of medical procedures and services from transparent providers across all specialties. Expands the traditional “in-network” concept marketed by large insurance carriers to a “non-network” concept that allows all providers to participate and without the cost of “in-network” OR the premium paid when you use your insurance card, which translates simply to better care at a lower cost.

Access

Membership is available to everyone in the U.S. regardless of age, pre-existing condition, or location. The value of the membership to the member is optimized when paired with high-deductible plans, health shares, or self-insurance; however, government sponsored plans and fully insured plans can see tremendous benefits as well.

Raise the expectations of U.S healthcare

High quality healthcare at a reasonable price (50% less than national average) with doctors who spend time with you and your family. Same or next day appointments, no wait times, in-person or virtual appointments, and modern technology. A clean, simple plan that allows you to see any doctor without the hassle of an insurance company determining care. Complete and comprehensive medical coverage to mitigate financial risk and ensure high quality care so you are never at-risk of medical bankruptcy while receiving care in a system designed to reward health. Transparent prices and quality of care data that allows you and your primary care doctor to make the best decisions on prescriptions, in/out patient procedures, hospitals, and emergency.

Does this sound too good to be true? It’s not.

We (patients, employers, providers) CAN reduce cost and improve quality of care by challenging the norms and assumptions about the U.S. healthcare system. All of the above and more is already in place, the key is to strip away all the unnecessary pieces that pervert the system and join together to fix our healthcare system. Separating the concept of health insurance and health care is key to changing the way we thinking about healthcare. Insurance is covering catastrophic financial risk, while care is what you receive to improve or sustain your health. Intertwining these two is one of the largest mistakes in human history and we must change course.
